- **bare-goal-complement-001 retracted (Brett).** There is no to-insertion blocking: to takes NP complements, not PP complements, so *go to home is ordinary categorial selection (go to work is fine), and go home is plain V + PP[goal] once home is a preposition. The entry was thin - its projections were properties of locative intransitive prepositions generally. Goal complementation is now an example with the correct explanation in intransitive-preposition-001; the preposition batch stands at category + intransitive use.
- **ago reanalyzed: obligatory pre-head modifier, not postposed complement (Brett).** The measure phrase in ten years ago is a pre-head modifier (as in ten years before I went), obligatory for ago (*I went ago); ago is an intransitive preposition. Postposed complements are a different, rarer pattern (the possibility of a veto notwithstanding). Fixed in preposition-001, intransitive-preposition-001, the ago override record, and the pos-defaults postposition rule note.
- **Intransitive means no object, for prepositions as for verbs (Brett).** The NP complement of a preposition bears the object function (the garden is the object of in; cf. CGELBank Head:P + Obj:NP). Clause-taking prepositions (because) are objectless and unthreatening to the definition. intransitive-preposition-001, preposition-001, and the outside override reworded.
- **Goal complement rebuilt on the coercion residue (Brett).** goal-complement-001: V + PP[goal], with to-PPs, directional PPs, and bare intransitive-P PPs (home, abroad) as equal realizations, and the constructional residue located where it actually is: manner-verb coercion into directed motion (Goldberg 1995 intransitive motion construction; The bottle floated into the cave). No blocking claims; *go to home is to's NP-only complementation. home fits as a bare-PP realization of the goal slot; way-construction and caused-motion flagged as future sister frames. First argument-structure construction in the repo.
